## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Regionalwerke Baden Builds In-House
**Likelihood**: 40%
**Impact**: Severe

**Mitigation**:
- Move faster than they can mobilize
- Build network effects (1,000 users = hard to compete)
- Demonstrate cost savings of acquisition vs. build
- Create technical complexity that requires 12+ months to replicate

### Risk 2: They Don't Want to Acquire (Strategic Misalignment)
**Likelihood**: 30%
**Impact**: High

**Mitigation**:
- Have alternative buyers lined up (Axpo, EKZ, Alpiq)
- Prove B2C business model works independently
- Offer licensing instead of acquisition
- Expand to adjacent territories (Wettingen, Brugg)

### Risk 3: Low Customer Adoption
**Likelihood**: 35%
**Impact**: Fatal

**Mitigation**:
- Set kill criteria: <200 registered addresses by Month 6 = pivot
- Focus on solar panel owners (highest motivation)
- Partner with installers who have direct customer access
- Aggressive early pricing (free formation for first 20 communities)

### Risk 4: Technical Execution Failure
**Likelihood**: 25%
**Impact**: High

**Mitigation**:
- Hire experienced contractor for critical features (billing, DSO integration)
- Start with MVP for each stage, iterate based on feedback
- Use proven open-source components where possible
- Budget 30% contingency for technical challenges

### Risk 5: Regulatory Changes
**Likelihood**: 10%
**Impact**: Severe

**Mitigation**:
- Monitor BFE announcements closely
- Build flexibility to pivot to ZEV/vZEV model
- Engage with Swiss energy policy advocates
- Diversify to B2B (utilities, municipalities) if B2C fails
